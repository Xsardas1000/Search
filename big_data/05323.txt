Abstract. Starting from the deï¬nition of Cheeger-Simons K-character given

1. Introduction

In the framework of type II superstring theory, there are two fundamental
pictures that describe and classify D-brane charges and the Ramond-Ramond ï¬elds.
The ï¬rst one relies on classical cohomology. In particular, a D-brane world-volume

Ramond-Ramond ï¬elds are classiï¬ed by ordinary diï¬€erential cohomology, for which
the Deligne cohomology provides a concrete model [2]. The Wess-Zumino action

particular, the Ramond-Ramond ï¬elds are classiï¬ed by a diï¬€erential K-theory class
[15, 13], while the topological charge of the D-brane is the corresponding K-theory
class. What we try to clarify in this paper is how to correctly deï¬ne the world-

map to diï¬€erential K-theory. In this way we are able to give a correct deï¬nition of

which we are able to compute the holonomy. We see that such a deï¬nition leads
to diï¬€erential K-characters, as deï¬ned in [1] and [6]. In this way we can draw a
complete parallel between the two classiï¬cation schemes. Since we consider ordinary
