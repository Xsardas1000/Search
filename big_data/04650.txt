Introduction

In this paper, we classify Lagrangian algebras in group-theoretical modular categories.
This, in particular, gives a classiï¬cation of physical modular invariants for group-theoretical
modular data (a problem raised in [2]). It should be mentioned that the set of labels for
physical modular invariants was obtained in [12] (using the language of module categories).
What was missing was a way of recovering the modular invariant corresponding to a label.
By establishing a correspondence between Lagrangian algebras and module categories, and
by computing the characters of Lagrangian algebras, we give a method for determining
modular invariants corresponding to module categories.

By a group-theoretical modular category Z(G, Î±), we mean the monoidal (or Drinfeld)
centre Z(V(G, Î±)) of the category of vector spaces V(G, Î±) graded by a ï¬nite group G.
Here, Î± is a 3-cocycle of G with coeï¬ƒcients in the multiplicative group kâˆ— of the ground
ï¬eld, which is used to twist the standard associativity constraint for the tensor product
of graded vector spaces. More precisely, we describe objects of Z(G, Î±) explicitly as G-
graded vector spaces with compatible G-action (section 2.1), and prove later (section 3.1)
that Z(G, Î±) is isomorphic to the monoidal centre Z(V(G, Î±)).

A commutative algebra A in a braided fusion category C is Lagrangian if any local
A-module in C is a direct sum of copies of A (we recall basic concepts of braided tensor
categories in section 1). We classify Lagrangian and more general indecomposable com-
mutative separable (etale for short) algebras in Z(G, Î±) in two steps. First, we describe
etale algebras with the trivial grading (section 2.3). These are nothing but indecomposable
commutative separable algebras with a G-action and hence are just function algebras on
transitive G-sets (we work over an algebraically closed ï¬eld k). Up to isomorphism, they
are labelled by (conjugacy classes of) subgroups H âŠ‚ G. Then we identify the category
of local modules Z(G, Î±)loc
k(G/H) with the group-theoretical modular category Z(H, Î±|H)
(theorem 2.10). A general etale algebra in Z(G, Î±) is an extension of its trivial degree
component, and hence is an etale algebra in one of Z(G, Î±)loc
k(G/H). Considered as an alge-
bra in Z(H, Î±|H), it has the one-dimensional trivial degree component. Our second step
(section 2.4) is to classify such algebras (proposition 2.15) and their categories of local
modules (theorem 2.17). Then in section 2.5, we combine the results obtaining the de-
scription of all etale algebras in Z(G, Î±) (theorem 2.19) and their local modules (theorem
2.20). As a corollary, we get a classiï¬cation of Lagrangian algebras in Z(G, Î±) (corollary
2.21). They are parametrised by (conjugacy classes of) subgroups H âŠ‚ G together with a
coboundary d(Î³) = Î±|H matching with the answer from [12]. In section 3, we explain this
matching by identifying Lagrangian algebras with full centres of indecomposable separable
algebras in V(G, Î±) (theorem 3.2). Finally, after recalling the character theory for objects
of Z(G, Î±) (section 4.1), we compute characters of Lagrangian algebras (theorem 4.8). We
treat the case G = S3 (the symmetric group on 3 symbols) as an example (section 4.3).

This paper extends the results of [3] to the case of Z(G, Î±) with a nontrivial cocycle Î±.
