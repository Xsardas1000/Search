Abstract. Let L be a closed, orientable, monotone Lagrangian 3-manifold of a symplectic
manifold (M, Ï‰), for which there exists a local system such that the corresponding La-
grangian quantum homology vanishes. We show that its cohomology ring satisï¬es a certain
dichotomy, which depends only on the parity of the ï¬rst Betti number of L. Essentially, the
triple cup product on the ï¬rst cohomology group is shown to be either of maximal rank or
identically zero. This in turn inï¬‚uences the Landau-Ginzburg superpotential of L: either
one of its partial derivatives do not vanish on the corresponding local system, or it is globally
constant. We use this to prove that quantum Reidemeister torsion is invariant and can be
expressed in terms of open Gromov-Witten invariants of L.

1. Introduction and results

Given a closed monotone Lagrangian manifold L of a symplectic manifold (M, Ï‰), one can
wonder how restricted its topology can be. Since every closed manifold is a Lagrangian of its
cotangent bundle (the zero-section), some restrictions must be imposed on M or L in order
to get non-trivial results. Without restricting M, one can impose that L is displaceable by
some Hamiltonian isotopy, wich implies that its Lagrangian quantum homology QH(L), in
the sense of Biran-Cornea [BC09b], vanishes. However, since the work of Cho [Cho08], a
milder restriction can be imposed. Indeed, Lagrangian quantum homology can be twisted

Ï•-narrow. These pairs (L, Ï•) are called trivial objects of the (monotone) Fukaya category

1.2. Examples. The most interesting conclusion of Theorem A concerns Lagrangians with
an even ï¬rst Betti number. Unfortunately, we have to confess that the only examples of this
type we could ï¬nd are rational homology spheres. It seems that real parts of Fano 3-folds
may provide a source of non trivial examples, see the discussion below.

1.2.1. Odd ï¬rst Betti number. These are rather easy to come by. Indeed, let S1 be a con-
tractible, monotone, circle in a Riemann surface (Î£, Ï‰1), possibly not compact. Let Î£g be

product embedding S1 Ã— Î£g âŠ‚ (Î£ Ã— M, Ï‰1 âŠ• Ï‰2) admits a narrow representation and has

1.2.2. Even ï¬rst Betti number. The Chiang Lagrangian [Chi04] is a rational homology sphere
in CP 3 with minimal Maslov class two.
Its Lagrangian quantum homology was studied
extensively by Evans-Lekili [EL15] and vanishes over ï¬elds of characteristic zero, hence the
hypotheses of Theorem A are satisï¬ed. Other Chiang-type examples were further studied
by Smith [Smi].

There are many closed orientable 3-manifolds L with even ï¬rst Betti numbers whose
cohomology ring satisï¬es IC = 0. For example, any orientable circle bundle with a non-
vanishing Euler class, or more generally, Seifert ï¬bered spaces with a non-vanishing Euler
class. These can also be made Lagrangian, albeit not monotone, as real parts of complex
3-folds, see Mangolte [Man14, Theorems 2.13 & 4.6] for an extensive survey. However,
the methods mentionned there do not seem to work for Fano 3-folds, whose real parts
would yield monotone Lagrangians. One would then need to check that they admit narrow
representations, so that the hypotheses of Theorem A are satisï¬ed. We will not pursue this
direction here.
