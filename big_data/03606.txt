Movability and co-movability of shape morphisms

P.S.Gevorgyan and I.Pop

Abstract

The purpose of this paper is to deï¬ne some notions of movability for mor-
phisms of inverse systems which extend the movability properties of inverse sys-
tems and which are compatible with the equivalence relations which deï¬ne pro-
morphisms and shape morphisms. Some properties, examples and applications
are given.

Introduction

The notion of movability for metric compacta was introduced by K. Borsuk [2] as an
important shape invariant. The movable spaces are a generalization of spaces having
the shape of ANRâ€™s. The movability assumption allows a series of important results in
algebraic topology (like the Whitehead and Hurewicz theorems) to remain valid when
the homotopy pro-groups are replaced by the corresponding shape groups. The term
â€movabilityâ€ comes from the geometric interpretation of the deï¬nition in the compact
case:
if X is a compactum lying in a space M âˆˆ AR, one says that X is movable if
for every neighborhood U of X in M there exists a neighborhood V âŠ‚ U of X such
that for every neighborhood W âŠ‚ U of X there is a homotopy H : V Ã— [0, 1] â†’ U
such that H(x, 0) = x and H(x, 1) âˆˆ W for every x âˆˆ V . One shows that the choice of
M âˆˆ AR is irrelevant [2]. After the notion of movability had been expressed in terms of
ANR-systems, for arbitrary topological spaces, [10], [11], it became clear that one could
deï¬ne it in arbitrary pro-categories. The deï¬nition of a movable object in an arbitrary
pro-category and that of uniform movability were both given by Maria MoszyÂ´nska
[13]. Uniform movability is important in the study of mono- and epi-morphisms in
pro-categories and in the study of the shape of pointed spaces. In the book of Sibe
MardeË‡siÂ´c and Jack Segal [11] all these approaches and applications of various types of
movability are discussed.

Some categorical approaches to movability in shape theory were given by P.S.Gevorgyan

[7], I. Pop [14], P.S. Gevorgyan & I. Pop [8], T.A. Avakyan & P.S. Gevorgyan [1].

The approach of this paper started from the idea that some results in shape theory,
valid in some conditions of movability for inverse systems or shape objects, could be
obtained in weak conditions. More precisely, the idea was to deï¬ne some movability
notions for the morphisms of inverse systems, so as, when these morphisms are identity
morphisms, the deï¬nitions of the usual movability properties to be found both for pro-
objects and shape objects. Besides the classic case of movability pro-objects and shape
objects, some notions of movability appear in the papers of T. Yagasaki about the study
of the shape ï¬brations [18] and [19], in a paper of Z. Ë‡Cerin on Lefschetz movable maps
[3], and in a paper of D. A. Edwards and P. Tulley MacAuley about a shape theory of
a map [6]. Unfortunately, these approaches are just particular cases and they do not
deal with the movability of shape morphisms in the general case of an abstract shape
theory. But the second author of this paper already approached this problem in two
previous article [15] and [16] in which some properties of movability for morphisms of
inverse systems and for pro-morphisms were deï¬ned and studied. But those properties
of movability could not be extended to shape morphisms and maps. In the present paper
this problem is solved by a slight but useful modiï¬cation of the notions of movability
and uniform movability for morphisms of inverse systems previously proposed, the
new deï¬nitions becoming compatible with the relations which deï¬ne the pro-morphism
and shape morphism. In this way we obtain some satisfactory notions of movability
(simple, strong or uniform) for shape morphisms and particularly for maps. Some
â€dualâ€ notions, namely that of co-movability (simple, strong or uniform), are deï¬ned.
Some properties, examples and applications are given.
